Sydney Gautreau,

Editor & Writing Coach

Ensure the quality of your ideas is reflected in the quality of your writing

My childhood nickname was Hector Corrector.

Unsurprisingly, the journey of my life has been learning how to channel my insufferable know-it-all energy into editorial expertise. Once I learned to harness and plumb the depths of my seemingly endless curiosity, I found that I had a singular knack for helping writers deepen their thinking and elevate the quality and coherence of their work.

For many authors, working with an editor is an exercise in vulnerability: allowing someone else into the worlds and ideas and arguments that you've spent days—in some cases, even years—carefully cultivating. I do not take this level of trust lightly; being a permitted guest into authors' writing processes is my greatest source of professional joy.

Some particulars about me:

I started editing informally in high school, formally in university, and professionally since graduating.

I completed my Honours Bachelor of Arts at the University of Toronto, where I graduated with High Distinction (Summa Cum Laude for the Americans) in History and English Literature. During my time there, I was published in multiple University journals and awarded the Canadian Labour Congress Book Prize.
